# Sergio Busquets — Post #2

### _"The Pass That Lets the Pass Happen."_

Sergio Busquets is the player you cannot measure as an outlier. On every
conventional axis — volume, completion, progression, press-resistance,
centrality — he is elite but never first, fourth to eighth, season after
season (`figures/05_never_first.png`). His greatness reads as **invariance**:
completion under pressure sat flat at ~85% for eleven straight seasons while
Xavi, Iniesta, Neymar and Mascherano left and the team was rebuilt around him
(`figures/01_the_constant.png`). Analytics rank what players do at their best;
Busquets's value is the thing that never goes wrong, and a metric for "never
goes wrong" looks like a non-finding.

So this analysis builds an instrument for the thing his role actually
performs: the **third-man connector** — a completed *simple, non-progressive*
pass whose recipient's very next ball is the unlock (a progression, a
final-third entry, a shot assist). The progressive-pass and assist boards are
blind to it by construction. On that one board he is **#1 of 45 La Liga
central midfielders in 2015/16** (`figures/08_third_man.png`), and the crown
survives a falsification battery: it holds *over expected* (not a by-product
of his pass volume), it survives controlling for the recipient and the zone,
and it survives deleting Messi **and** Iniesta from every chain — still Barça's
best. The showpiece is the receiver uplift: the same world-class teammates
become measurably more dangerous *specifically after his feed*
(`figures/09_receiver_uplift.png`).

And it never moved. The same metric crowns him top-three among the Barça
midfielders on show in **all thirteen** cached seasons, flat at ~10 per 90
(`figures/10_persistence.png`) — the crown and the invariance are the same
number. But honesty is the product here, so the analysis states the boundary
plainly: **the crown does not travel.** With Spain it weakens sharply — rank
10 of 23, where ball-playing centre-backs and Rodri own the role instead. The
claim is therefore precise. Busquets was the engine of an invisible mechanism
*inside the system built for it* — not a lone genius who tops any board
anywhere. That marriage of player and positional-play ecosystem is the
finding.

Every claim in the post cites a figure generated by code in this repo.

## Reproduce the figures

Run from the repo root (regenerates all 10 PNGs from cached data):

```bash
.venv/Scripts/python -X utf8 players/sergio-busquets/run_all.py
```

The figures read pre-computed caches. The `_pull_*.py` / `_build_axes.py`
helpers warm the StatsBomb cache for figures 01–07; the `_third_man_*.py`
falsification battery writes the boards behind figures 08–10. They run once
and persist their output to `data/processed/`.

## What's here

- `notebooks/` — 10 numbered scripts that build the figures, plus shared
  helpers: `_chains.py` (the enriched pass-chain backbone every third-man test
  reads off), the `_pull_*.py` / `_build_axes.py` cache warmers, and the
  `_third_man_*.py` falsification battery (over-expected and recipient
  fixed-effects robustness, 13-season replication, and the out-of-club Spain
  comparison).
- `figures/` — the 10 publication PNGs.
